The Detected Filterbank archive data product is a dynamic spectra; that is, a time series of spectra (also known as ‘waterfall’ data). Spectra are formed by detecting (squaring) and time-averaging channelized voltage data. The spectral resolution depends upon the channel width, and the temporal resolution depends upon how many time samples are averaged together.

Detected Filterbank data contain quasi-Stokes I,Q,U and V coherency products. The Detected Filterbank file can optionally have phase-coherent dedispersion applied (to remove the effect of dispersion due to signal propagation through electrons in the interstellar medium) and phase-coherent correction of Faraday rotation applied (to correct polarization rotation due to propagation through magnetic fields). RFI mitigation/flagging may also be applied.

The Detected Filterbank data product can be considered as a N-dimensional array with:

  • Shape:
  • Datatype: integer
  • Bitdepth: 1,2, 4, 8, 16 or 32 bits.

Data formats

Detected filterbank data are stored in the PSRFITS file format [RD9], along with statistics stored in a custom HDF5-based file format.
