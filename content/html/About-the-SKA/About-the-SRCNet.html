The SKA Regional Centre Network (SRCNet)

The SKA Telescopes will produce immense amounts of data - up to 700 PB per year with a single SDP data product being up to 2PB alone. This makes the traditional download-and-analyse-locally model unfeasible for SKA data. The data delivered by the SKAO will not be in a final, publishable format in the majority of cases. Further processing and user-led data reduction, analysis, and modelling will be needed. Added to this the user base and individual project teams who will work on SKA data are distributed across the planet.

To address the needs of the users and maximise the scientific productivity and impact of the SKA, a collaborative and federated network of SKA Regional Centres, globally distributed across SKA partner countries, is being developed: the SRCNet. The SRCNet will host the SKA Science Archive, make data storage, processing, and collaboration spaces available, as well as support and train the community.

SKA Regional Centres

SKA Regional Centres (SRCs) are regionally funded virtual entities offering data access and processing resources for use by SKA users. SRCs collaborate to form a global network that provides the SKA community with:

  • access to support using the SKA and its data products,
  • a platform for collaborative science,
  • transparent and location agnostic interface for users,
  • access to project data for all SKA users,
  • a place for development of software tools: analysis, modelling, visualisation.

Access to the SRCNet

Users will be allocated resources on the SRCNet to carry out their analyses, additional processing, and modelling of SKA data (proprietary or publicly available archive data) to achieve their project’s science goals. Users will access their data (or search the archive) and compute resources via the SKA Science Gateway, which also provides science-enabling tools and applications and template workflows that could be used as-is or as a basis for a bespoke workflow. Users will also have the capability to upload advanced data products [link TBD] produced elsewhere in pursuit of their science goals for archiving purposes.

Users (regardless of their geographic location, that of the SRC staging their data, and whether their country hosts an SRC) will access their data and processing allocations in the same way - via the SKA Science Gateway.
