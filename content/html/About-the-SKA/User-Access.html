Getting Data

To get access to data from either or both SKA Telescopes, you have three options:

  • If the data you need has not already been collected as part of another project, you will need to submit a proposal for observing time and SRCNet resources at the next appropriate Call for Proposals (or via Directors Discretionary Time).
  • For data that has been collected as part of another project, you will need to - if required - submit an archive project proposal for SRCNet resources and wait for the data to be out of its proprietary period (typically one year, but TBC). If no SRCNet resources are required, data that is outside of its proprietary period is public and can be downloaded from the Science Archive.
  • Science Verification data will not have a proprietary period, and therefore will be completely public. Ideas for Science Verification targets can be submitted at the Call for Ideas approximately six months prior to the start of Science Verification for each telescope and phase. Requests for SRCNet resources to process Science Verification Data can be made via <whatever mechanism it is we are going to be using>.

Proposals

Calls for PI Proposals<link to page> are expected annually in standard operations, occurring 6 months prior to the start of the next observing cycle (one observing cycle is 12 months). A Call for Proposals will include a complete list of available modes and capabilities for that cycle. Key Science Calls for Proposals are expected every 3 years.

Proposals will be submitted via the Proposal Preparation Tool and will include scientific and technical justifications, observation specifications, and information on SRCNet resource requirements, as well as the proposal types and attributes of the project (e.g. Key Science Projects, PI Projects, Target of Opportunity – for more information see XYZ). Observatory tools [link TBD] are provided to aid teams in the preparation of their proposals and estimation of resources.

Proposals will be subject to merit-based assessment via an independent Time Allocation Committee supported by dual anonymous distributed peer review[link TBD].

Time Allocation and Member Shares

Observing time will be allocated on the basis of scientific merit (as evaluated by the Time Allocation Committee) and technical feasibility, while maintaining this access in proportion to member shares. A fundamental principle of the SKAO is that access to SKA resources will be in proposition to a member country’s share in the SKA Project, so if there is a sustained imbalance over a number of cycles, targeted intervention may be considered. A small amount of Open Time (exact amount TBD) will be made available during standard operations. More information on how Member Share accounting works and how observing time is attributed to members can be found <link XYZ>.

Data Product Access

Due to the magnitude of data being generated and delivered by the SKA Telescopes, calibrated Observatory Data Products will be automatically generated by the Observatory and delivered to users (via the SKA Regional Centre Network, SRCNet) with a likely proprietary period of one year. Raw visibility data will not routinely be delivered. Access to and further processing of these data products will be via the SRCNet. For more information about data products see <XYZ>.

Data Restrictions

During the proprietary period, data access is restricted to a project team who are granted rights to use the data to achieve their approved project goals. Serendipitous discoveries are expected and publication of serendipitous discoveries is encouraged as long as it does not impinge on the science goals of other projects using data derived from the same observations (due to commensal observing - see XYZ for more information) and still within their proprietary period. To remove the risk of accidental impingement on other teams’ science goals, the Director-General’s permission is required to publish results that differ from a project’s approved science goals. See XYZ for further information.

Observatory Data Products and team-selected advanced data products [link TBD] will be made available to the public at the end of the proprietary period.

SRCNet Resources

A project team will be allocated a specific amount of SRCNet resources, sufficient to process the data for their project goals, whether this is for newly acquired data or as an archive project. Teams should be conscious of the resources being used for processing their data to avoid “burning through” their allocation as additional resources will not be available.
